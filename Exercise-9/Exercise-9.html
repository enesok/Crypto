  1. Problem: AE-security: simple examples (Exercise 9.1 in BS)

    Let \((E, D)\) be an AE-secure cipher. Consider the following derived ciphers:

    1. \(E_1(k,m) := (E(k,m), E(k,m))\); \(D_1(k, (c_1, c_2)) := \begin{cases} D(k,c_1) & \text{if $D(k,c_1) = D(k,c_2)$ }\\ {\bf reject}& \text{otherwise}\end{cases}\)

    2. \(E_2(k,m) := \{c \xleftarrow{}E(k,m), output(c,c) \}\); \(D_2(k, (c_1, c_2)) := \begin{cases} D(k,c_1) & \text{if $c_1 = c_2$ }\\ {\bf reject}& \text{otherwise}\end{cases}\)

    Show that part (b) is AE-secure, but part (a) is not.
